# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Technical colleagues and peers — developers with some ML grounding — watching a
live talk titled *"Sotto il Cofano degli LLM"* ("Under the Hood of LLMs"). It is
a projected, keyboard/scroll-driven slide deck (Italian language) presented in a
room, not read alone. The audience can follow technical depth but wants the
mechanism made legible, not hand-waved.

## Product Purpose

Take a technical audience past the "perceived magic" of Large Language Models and
give them genuine, step-by-step understanding of what actually happens under the
hood — from the 1943 neuron to the token generated an instant ago. Success is the
audience leaving able to reason about how an LLM works, not just what it outputs.

## Brand Personality

Confident and precise. Engineering-grade clarity: sharp, technical, no fluff —
the diagrams carry the explanation. Voice is direct and assured, the tone of
someone who knows the material cold and respects the audience's intelligence.
Three words: **precise, lucid, unshowy**.

## Anti-references

- **Generic corporate PowerPoint** — bullet-point walls, clip-art, stock-photo
  gradients, template chrome.
- **AI-slop SaaS landing** — gradient text everywhere, identical icon-card grids,
  tiny tracked uppercase eyebrows on every section, decorative glassmorphism.
- **Overstimulating / flashy** — motion for its own sake, neon, animation that
  competes with the content instead of revealing it.
- **Childish / playful** — cartoonish illustration, bubbly rounded everything,
  emoji-heavy.

## Design Principles

1. **The diagram is the argument.** Each slide earns its place by making one
   mechanism visible. If a custom SVG/visual explains it better than prose, the
   visual leads and text annotates — never the reverse.
2. **One idea per slide.** Depth comes from sequence, not density. Resist
   cramming; let the deck's progression do the teaching.
3. **Projection-legible by default.** It's shown on a screen across a room:
   contrast, type size, and figure weight must read from the back row, not just
   on a laptop.
4. **Motion reveals, never decorates.** Animation exists to stage an idea (build a
   diagram, sequence a flow). A reveal that adds no meaning is cut.
5. **Earn every flourish.** Reflexes like gradient text, numbered/tracked
   eyebrows on every section, and identical card grids are the AI-slop tells we
   reject — use a device only where it carries information.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No hard accessibility requirements specified. Still, because the deck is
projected, hold a practical floor: high foreground/background contrast that
survives a projector and bright rooms, legible type sizes, and a reduced-motion
fallback so animations never become a barrier.
